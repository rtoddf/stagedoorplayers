The Cast of The 39 Steps

George Contini

George Contini is an Associate Professor in the University of Georgia's Theatre and Film Studies Department where he specializes in Characterization (Michael Chekhov), Acting on Camera, Solo Performance, and Queer Theatre and Film. In addition to teaching full time, George maintains a career as an actor, director, and playwright. Most recently he appeared in The Explorer's Club at the Aurora Theatre which received a Suzi Bass Award nomination for Best Ensemble. His original solo show ManActress (formerly Put It In the Scrapbook) has toured to the Chicago Fringe Festival and the New Orleans Fringe Festival. Other Atlanta credits include directing Don’t Dress for Dinner at the Aurora Theatre and the South Regional Premiere of Assistance at Pinch N Ouch Theatre. He was seen as Reverend Howard Finster in the World Premiere of Hidden Man at 7Stages. Prior to that he was seen in Shear Madness at Alliance Theatre and The Big Bang at the Horizon Theatre.

Stephanie Friedman

Stephanie Friedman Stage Door Debut! Atlanta Credits: Stupid F***king Bird, The Whale, Pluto, Spring Awakening (Actor's Express) The Unauthorized…Sam Brown (Aurora Theatre), Charley's Aunt (Georgia Ensemble Theatre), Titus Clown (Out of Hand), Philadelphia, Here I Come (Aris). Regional: Tonya & Nancy: the Rock Opera (ART – Club Oberon), The Wild Place (Underground Railway – Cambridge). BFA from Boston University’s College of Fine Arts.

Tony Larkin

Tony Larkin is so happy to be back at Stage Door, having previously appeared in Miracle on South Division Street and It's a Wonderful Life: A Live Radio Play. Besides having worked regionally at theatres in Boston, MA, Asheville, NC, Bangor, ME, and Highlands, NC, in Atlanta Tony has performed with The Alliance, Aurora, Theatrical Outfit, Actor's Express, Serenbe Playhouse, Georgia Ensemble Theatre, Synchronicity, Dad's Garage, and many others. Huge thanks and love to Robert, Hayley, George, and this fantastic cast. 

John Markowski

John Markowski is thrilled to be returning to the Stage Door Players family! Other Stage Door Players credits include The Game's Afoot, Lend Me A Tenor (Woodie Award winner- Best Actor), Unnecessary Farce, Moon Over Buffalo, and Leading Ladies. John has performed around Atlanta with the Aurora Theatre, Atlanta Lyric Theatre, The Process Theatre Company, and Onstage Atlanta. John holds a BA in Theatre: Performance from The College of Charleston and is currently a full time employee with Kaiser Permanente's Educational Theatre Programs of Georgia as a teaching artist. Many thanks to this ridiculously funny and talented cast, George Contini, Robert Egizio, Hayley, and the hard working crew and design team. Love always to the Markowski clan. 

Ryan Stillings

Ryan Stillings is proud to be with Stage Door Players again after working with them in Southern Comforts. Ryan got his start in Atlanta as an intern for Serenbe Playhouse, where he performed in The Velveteen Rabbit and Hair the musical. Since then he has been in 42nd Street as Pat Denning and in Mary Poppins as Mr Northbrook. He recently moved to Atlanta again after working with Tweetsie Railroad as one of their cowboys, and he couldn't be happier to start in career here.

Jillian Walzer

Jillian is delighted to be a part of her first production with Stage Door Players. By day Jillian can be found working as an Operations Coordinator at Northside Hospital Cancer Institute.  Among her numerous stage and screen credits, her favorite roles on stage include Tina in Tony & Tina's Wedding, Maggie Saunders in Lend Me A Tenor, Mary in Laughing Stock as well as producing and performing in Vagina Monologues.  Jillian can be seen next in Onion Man Productions, Summer Harvest Play Festival, and she is thrilled to be amongst such a talented cast and crew.  A special thank you to Robert....Timing is everything!

Jacob York

A native of Benton, Kentucky, Jacob is excited to be back on stage here at SDP, where he is an artistic associate. At Stage Door, Jacob has been featured in The Game's Afoot, It's a Wonderful Life: A Live Radio Play and Unnecessary Farce. Some of his favorites at other theatres include The Importance of Being Earnest (Nashville Repertory Theatre), Fat Pig (Aurora Theatre), Macbeth (Atlanta Shakespeare Company), and the world premier Angry Fags (7 Stages).  You might also be familiar with Jacob's work on TV (Turn on AMC, Your Pretty Face is Going to Hell on Adult Swim) or film (Paternity Leave, What's the Matter with Gerald?). If you want to know more about Jacob, feel free to check out his Facebook actor page at tinyurl.com/jacobyork
