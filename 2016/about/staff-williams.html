John David Williams

Lighting Designer in Residence

JD is a native Georgian and Dunwoody resident who joined the Stage Door Players for the 2008-2009 season. He got his start designing lights at South Georgia College using equipment that looked like it was installed when the school was chartered in 1906 and is happy to be working with much newer controls and fixtures here.

Prior to joining the, JD has served as technical director for Galaxy Music Theater and lighting director for Neighborhood Playhouse. JD has also designed shows for Onstage Atlanta and Macon Little Theatre. He has received numerous design awards around Atlanta since moving to the area in 1990. His first design for SDP was for Wrong Turn at Lungfish. Favorite shows here include Master Class, Pippin (co-design with Michael W. Magursky), Six Dance Lessons in Six Weeks and Visiting Mister Green. Other designs include Dream Girls and Smokey Joe's Cafe with Galaxy Music Theatre, Angels in America, K2 and Titanic (in concert) at Onstage Atlanta, Into the Woods and Singinâ€™ in the Rain at Neighborhood Playhouse.

Also a percussionist, JD has played in the pit for several shows here at SDP. He has been seen and heard performing throughout the Southeastern US in major malls, parades and wedding receptions from Charleston, SC to Jackson, MS with The Seed & Feed Marching Abominable. He also works with direct marketing databases and is "an expert" in arcane USPS Postal Regulations.
