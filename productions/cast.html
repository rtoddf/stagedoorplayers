The Cast of Let Nothing You Dismay

Bryan Brendle

Bryan Brendle (Dennis, Jerry, Rich) is tickled pink to be back at Stage Door! And at the holidays! He previously appeared in Same Time, Next Year and The Games Afoot or Holmes for the Holidays. Around town, Bryan has performed at Actor's Express, Atlanta Lyric, ART Station, Aurora, Georgia Ensemble, Horizon, OnStage Atlanta, Pinch n Ouch, Serenbe, and Theatre du Reve. Some of his film and TV credits include: Powers, Bloodlands, Big Losers, That's Educational (web series), 42, Necessary Roughness, Franklin & Bash, Drop Dead Diva, Army Wives, One Life to Live, and Rhythm of the Saints(Sundance Film Festival). Bryan received a BFA in Acting from the Florida State University. He'd like to thank Topher, Robert, Shannon, Hayley and the entire cast and crew for the opportunity and his daughter Alexandra for her love, light and support. And thank you for supporting live theatre!

Amanda Cucher

Amanda Cucher (Kaitlyn, Brianna, Angela) is delighted to return to Stage Door Players since her previous Woodie Award winning performance in Sylvia, as well as Madge Geisel in The Game’s Afoot, or Holmes for the Holidays, Dana in Beached Wails, Randi in Greetings, and Traci in Spreading it Around. Other local theatrical endeavors include Brooke Wyeth in Other Desert Cities and Sister James in Doubt at Out of Box Theater, Evelyn in Evelyn in Purgatory with Essential Theatre Company and Barbara Grant in Perfect Arrangement with Process Theatre Company She would like to thank Shannon and Robert, this amazing cast, and Topher.

Mark Gray

Mark is honored to be returning to Stage Door Players. You might remember Mark from the SDP productions of It’s A Wonderful Life A Radio Play, Lend Me A Tenor and Blythe Spirit. Additional theatre credits include 1:23 for Synchronicity Theatre as well as Based on a Totally True Story, Bent, Gypsy, Bee Luther Hatchee, Doll Plays and Auntie Mame all for Actors Express. Marks television credits include Showtime’s Comedy Club Network, Days of Our Lives on NBC and One West Waikiki for ABC. Mark has also been featured in the films World Traveler, Bobby Jones - Stroke of Genius and Tyler Perry’s Meet The Browns. A big thank you to Robert, Shannon and Topher for this chance to play with an amazing group of artists. Saving the biggest thanks for Virgia, his wife for more than 32 years for agreeing to be a theatre widow – once again.

Shelly McCook

Shelly McCook (Noreen/Charlotte/Bubbie) Shelly is so happy to be back with Robert, Hayley and the Stage Door Players gang after directing Sylvia last summer. Shelly is has performed or directed on most of the Atlanta professional stages including Georgia Ensemble, the late Theatre in the Square, Theatrical Outfit, 7Stages, Actors Express, Aurora, Horizon and the Alliance Theatre. She is an acting coach and teacher and will be teaching comedy classes for both adults and teens at Elm Street Arts Village this summer.  

Doyle Reynolds

Doyle Reynolds (Ivan, Milford, Oscar) is very happy to be back at Stage Door and working with this incredibly funny and talented group of people. He was just here this past summer in "Sylvia" (Woodie Award & Suzi Bass Nomination). He is an Associate Artist with Actor’s Express, where he has performed in "6 Degrees of Separation," "100 Saints You Should Know," "Finn In The Underworld," "Some Men (Suzi Bass Nomination)," "I Am My Own Wife" (Suzi Bass Award) and "Rescue and Recovery." At Geva Theatre and Aurora Theatre: "Bob." At Synchronicity: "The Vibrator Play." At 7 Stages: "Mr. Universe" and "The Violet Hour." At Theatre In The Square: "Dirty Blonde," "Sylvia," "Gross Indecency, The 3 Trials of Oscar Wilde," and "Red Scare On Sunset."  Other theatre credits include extensive work with Dad’s Garage Theatre, as well as work with Pinch ‘n Ouch, Jewish Theatre of The South, Horizon Theatre Company, Soul-stice Repertory Theatre, barking dog theatre and others.  Thank you for supporting live theatre!

Gina Rickicki

Gina Rickicki (Tawny, Deb, Linda) has been bringing her bold character work to the stages of Atlanta for a few years now after relocating from NYC. She's so happy to return to Stage Door since her turn as Maggie Mundy in Dancing at Lughnasa! You may have also seen her in Hot Pink: Or Ready to Explode (Weird Sisters), Brick By Brick We Will Tear These Walls Apart (Dismantle Theatre), or King of Pops:The Musical (Dad's Garage). Maybe you caught her at one of Atlanta's many live literature events such as Write Club, or her own monthly show, Naked City (at the Goat Farm). It's possible you know her from her work on her web series Ask Hatter and Hare (we're on the Facebooks). Maybe you've never seen her before, in which case, she hopes to make you laugh a bunch and likely wonder how she got so strange.

Emily Sams

Emily Sams (Allie, Lizzie) is absolutely thrilled to be performing in her first production with Stage Door Players! She recently appeared at Out of Box Theater as Carly Simon in Shadows & Light, and as Jackie-O in House of Yes. Other past roles include Linda in The Wedding Singer, Maureen in RENT, and Lucy in You're a Good Man, Charlie Brown. HUGE thanks to her family, friends, and Dan for their love and encouragement. And to anyone reading this program, thank you for supporting the local arts!

Ben Silver

Ben Silver (Kevin/Leonard) is excited to make his Stage Door debut. He could not be more excited to be collaborating with Topher Payne on this incredible project. His Atlanta credits include Tuck Everlasting (Alliance Theatre)  Over The Rainbow, Six Degrees of Separation (Actor's Express) Lillian Likes It, Mysterious Connections (Essential Theatre) Assistance (PinchNOuch Theatre) and Romeo And Juliet (Oglethorpe Theatre)  Film: "Alone With People." He would like to thank his parents for their never-ending love, his mentor, Matt Huff and Jessica De Maria for all of her support.
