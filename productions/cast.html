The Cast of I Hate Hamlet

Robin Bloodworth

Robin is excited to be back with SDP after appearing in "Rounding Third" last year. Regional theater includes: "Clybourne Park", "End Days", "Voir Dire", "The Night of the Iguana", "Ah, Wilderness!", "Time Stands Still" (Suzi Award Winner), "The Time of Your Life", "Macbeth", "Fool for Love", "Art", "in the next room or The Vibrator Play", "Artist Descending a Staircase", the world premieres of "This Passion Thing", and "The Algae Eaters". In NY: "Piano Men", "Girl Science" (Ensemble Studio Theatre), "Cloud 9" (20th anniversary revival), "Much Ado About Nothing" (OOBR award winner NY Classical Theatre).

TV and Film credits include: Halt and Catch Fire, Your Worst Nightmare, Necessary Roughness, The Game, House of Payne, The View, Rescue 911, In the Heat of the Night, Midnight Edition, Talk Show and Day of Judgement.

Kathryn David

Kathryn David is thrilled to be making her Stage Door Debut. Favorite Credits Include: Luciana in Comedy of Errors (The Vineyard Playhouse), Olive in The 25th… Spelling Bee, and Liesl in The Sound of Music (Theatre Tuscaloosa). Atlanta Credits: The Gifts of the Magi (u/s, Theatrical Outfit), Ragtime (Atlanta Lyric). Commercial: Aaron’s, Roadie, Amazon. TV: Bloodlands (Investigation Discovery) BA: Musical Theatre, Birmingham-Southern College. @notkdave

Dan Ford

Dan Ford is ecstatic to return to Stage Door. His last role at Stage Door was playing Judas in Godspell. Other favorite credits include Marcellus in Hamlet at Georgia Shakespeare(U/S Hamlet 5 performances), Madame in The Maids for The Seedling Project, Robertson Aye in Mary Poppins and Feuilly in Les Mis at Aurora. Dan received a B.F.A in Performance at Webster University's Conservatory of theatre Arts. He would like extend a thousand thank you's to the family who took the time to come see him, the people and organizations who made this production possible, Robert and Jacob for all their support, and finally to Casey who is out on the road; I love you and miss you, Bunny!

Gina Rickicki

Gina Rickicki (Felecia Dantine) loves to make people laugh: she's been doing it through her character and improv work on various stages in Atlanta since relocating from NYC a few years ago. This year is already starting with a bang- being asked to do two shows in one Stage Door season is a rare treat! She just closed "Let Nothing You Dismay" here, and previously played Maggie in "Dancing at Lughnasa" at SDP. You may have also seen her in "Hot Pink: Or Ready to Explode" (Weird Sisters), "Brick By Brick We Will Tear These Walls Apart" (Dismantle Theatre), "The Doctor, The Devil, and My Dad" at 7 Stages, or "King of Pops:The Musical" (Dad's Garage). Maybe you caught her at one of Atlanta's many live literature events such as Write Club, or her own monthly show, Naked City (at the Goat Farm). Maybe you've never seen her before, in which case, she hopes to make you laugh a bunch and likely wonder how she got so strange. 

Holly Stevenson

Holly is thrilled to be returning to the Stage Door stage and past fave shows include The Games Afoot, Moon over Buffalo and Spreading It Around (2009 "Woodie Award" for Best Actress). Lately she has been growing her voiceover career and harmonizing with the John Godwin and Friends Band. Love and gratitude to J. Thanks to Ilse.

Jonathan D. Williams

Jonathan D. Williams (Gary) Ever since his second grade teacher forced him to perform in a school play as a bunny who "one day hoped to have ears", Jonathan has been performing.  Through High School and college he continued to pursue his love of theatre which paved the way for him to be involved in several plays and TV/Film productions in the Southeast. But nothing could make him more happy than having the opportunity to perform at Stage Door for the first time. Jonathan would like to thank the cast and crew of "I Hate Hamlet" and his family, Sunny, Dora, and Bobby for their unwavering support and encouragement
