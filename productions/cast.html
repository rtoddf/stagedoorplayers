The Cast of AIN'T MISBEHAVIN'

Matt Baum

Matt Baum has worked as an actor in Atlanta for nearly a decade. So far, he's had the privilege of working with nine different theaters in Atlanta. Stage Door makes it an even ten!

Erin Burnett

Erin Burnett is thrilled to be back at Stage Door Players after last appearing in It's a Wonderful Life: the Live Radio Play as Mary/Sally Applewhite. Other recent performances include: Footloose (Vi Moore) at Theatre Buford, Cat on a Hot Tin Roof (Maggie) at The Springer Opera House, and Titanic: the Musical (Madeline Astor) at The Serenbe Playhouse. Erin would like to thank her friends and family for their immense support, Robert and Kate for creating this fun, collaborative ensemble, and YOU for supporting live theatre!

Rachel Frawley

Rachel Frawley is an actor and producing artist with a B.F.A. in Theatre from Michigan State University. Producing credits include the 2016-2018 seasons for the Weird Sisters Theatre Project. Recent acting credits: The Secretaries at Out Front Theatre, Clue with Transgression and Deer Bear Wolf, Stage Door Players' Picnic, and Shitfaced Shakespeare's Romeo and Juliet. Coming up, The Three Musketeers at the Shakespeare Tavern. Thanks to family, ATL family, and the Stage Door family!

Mark Gray

Mark Gray is honored to be returning to Stage Door Players. Mark was last at SDP in Mass Appeal. You might remember Mark from other SDP productions of Suzi Award winning Let Nothing You Dismay, It's A Wonderful Life A Radio Play, Lend Me A Tenor and Blythe Spirit. Additional theatre credits include 1:23 for Synchronicity Theatre as well as Based on a Totally True Story, Bent, Gypsy, Bee Luther Hatchee, Doll Plays and Auntie Mame all for Actors Express. Marks television credits include Showtime's Comedy Club Network, Days of Our Lives on NBC and One West Waikiki for ABC. Mark has also been featured in the films World Traveler, Bobby Jones - Stroke of Genius and Tyler Perry's Meet The Browns. Mark sends thanks to his fellow cast members, who make up this lunatic asylum. A big thank you to Kate for seeing the moments of clarity through the lunacy. A big thank you to Robert for all that he has done for SDP and bringing quality theatre to Dunwoody. Saving the biggest thanks for Virgia, his wife for more than 35 years for agreeing to be a theatre widow – once again

Davin Allen Grindstaff

Davin Allen Grindstaff is excited to be returning to Stage Door Players.  He previously appeared in Dial M for Murder and Out of Order. You might have also seen his work at OutFront Theatre, Gypsy Theatre, Out of Box Theatre, and Onstage Atlanta. He won the 2014 M.A.T. Award for Best Actor in a Play (Allan Felix, Play It Again, Sam).  TV/film credits include: I, Tonya, Stan Against Evil, and Swamp Murders. When not on stage, Davin lives a double-life as a professor of Speech Communication at Georgia State University and a professional figure skater. Look for the pilot episode of his new comedy series, Walter Frisk Lets Go on YouTube. He thanks his parents for teaching him the value of doing what you love.

Shaun MacLean

Shaun MacLean is delighted to be back performing at Stage Door Players! Recent credits include: A Public Education (Out of Box Theatre), The Last Night of Ballyhoo (Stage Door Players), A Christmas Carol (Theatre Buford), The Pillowman (Independent Artists' Playhouse), Freaky Friday (Horizon Theatre), She Kills Monsters (Out of Box Theatre). See Shaun next in Theatrical Outfit's repertory production of Our Town and The Laramie Project; then over the holidays, catch him again at TO in The Wickhams: Christmas at Pemberley! Shaun would not have made it this far without unconditional love and support from Mom, Dad, Devin and Carleigh, all of his incredible friends, Mr. Rick Andosca, and the fine folks at People Store. Love y'all, enjoy the show!

Daniel Parvis

Daniel Parvis is thrilled to be making his debut with Stage Door Players. Some of his favorite roles include George in Of Mice and Men (Serenbe Playhouse), Mitch in A Streetcar Named Desire (Serenbe Playhouse), Caleb in The Whipping Man (The Warehouse Theatre, Greenville SC), and Henry VI in Henry VI: Parts I, II and III. (The Atlanta Shakespeare Company). He has also appeared onstage at Georgia Ensemble Theatre and Synchronicity Theatre. In film and television, he has appeared in projects such as “Sleepy Hollow” (FOX), “Watchmen” (HBO), Drop Dead Diva (Lifetime) and Being Mary Jane (BET). He received his BA in Theatre from University of Georgia in 2004, and he's been living and working in Atlanta ever since. He apprenticed with The Atlanta Shakespeare Company in 2005, and he performed numerous Shakespeare and non-Shakespeare roles over the following eight seasons. When Daniel's not acting, he's probably out shooting actor headshots, theatre production and/or promotional photography, or any other assorted assignments that come his way as owner and operator of Daniel Parvis Photography. Otherwise, he's probably at the dog park, strumming some tunes, or tearing up the town on his motorbike (weather permitting…or not). Love to Katie.

Doyle Reynolds

Doyle Reynolds is elated to be back at Stage Door where he's appeared in Dial M for Murder, Let Nothing You Dismay and Sylvia. Credits include: The Book of Will (Theatrical Outfit); Nell Gwynn, Anne Boleyn, The Vibrator Play (Synchronicity); 6 Degrees of Separation, 100 Saints You Should Know, Finn In the Underworld, Some Men, I Am My Own Wife (Actor's Express); Bob (Geva Theatre and Aurora Theatre); Mr. Universe, The Violet Hour (7 Stages). Other credits include plays with Dad's Garage Theatre, Theatre in the Square, Pinch ‘n Ouch, and many others.  Thank you for supporting live theatre!
