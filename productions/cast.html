The Cast of A RED PLAID SHIRT

Steve Hudson

Steve Hudson is delighted to make his Stage Door Players debut in A Red Plaid Shirt! He most recently was seen at the Atlanta Lyric Theatre in both Tarzan and Dirty Rotten Scoundrels. Other recent credits include The Comedy of Errors at the Shakespeare Tavern, The Hunchback of Notre Dame at Aurora Theatre and Theatrical Outfit, and Company at Actor's Express. Some of his favorite roles are Stephen Spettigue in Charley's Aunt (Georgia Ensemble Theatre), Patsy in The New Electric Ballroom (Aris Theatre), Man in Chair in The Drowsy Chaperone (Aurora Theatre), and Schultz in Circle Mirror Transformation (Theatre in the Square). Proverbs 9:10

Eileen Koteles

Eileen Koteles is very excited to be making her debut performance at Stage Door Players! Previous credits include: Barefoot in the Park, Wisdom of Eve, The Savannah Sipping Society, Hats The Musical, Grease, Mame, King and I, South Pacific, All My Sons, Agnes of G-d, Sister's Rosensweig, for which she received Creative Loafing's Best Actress Award and Veronica's Room, for which she received the Best Actress Critics Award. Eileen has spent much of her life dancing, and choreographing musicals and reviews: Oklahoma, Dames at Sea, Guys and Dolls, Fiddler on the Roof, Beethoven's Nightclub Reviews, Glory Days (MJCC), and My Fair Lady (DUMC).

Suzanne Jordan Roush

Suzanne Jordan Roush is delighted to be part of this lovely production with these attractive and witty people. Suzanne joined the Atlanta theatre community back in 1986, when she moved to Atlanta to be a member of the Alliance Theatre Acting Intern program. Since then, she has been seen on stages at the Alliance Children's Theatre, Horizon Theatre, Marietta Theatre in the Square, GA Shakespeare Tavern, GA Shakespeare Festival, Jewish Theatre of the South, GA Ensemble, Theatrical Outfit and Stage Door Players. Most recently she appeared in Built to Float at the Essential Theatre. Love to Steve and Lili.

Michael Strauss

So… What were we talking about? It seems like I was just here. I was last seen on these boards as Maestro Vito De Angelis back in June. I'm delighted to be back so soon. You may have seen me around town as Judge Turpin in Sweeney Todd (Actor's Express), Signor Naccarelli in The Light In The Piazza (Theatrical Outfit), Reginald Herz in The Meantime or Cord Elam in Oklahoma (Theater Emory), Hank Adams, the lonely cop in Christmas Wishes (ArtStation), Capt. Von Trapp (Center Theatre) or Harry, the inebriated brother of the dead Senate candidate in Lying In State (GET). Recent history also has me performing a quartet of shows at The Alhambra in Jacksonville and a plum role at the Arts Center of Coastal Carolina on Hilton Head Island. Thank you to my wife Valory for all that she is, to Robert for having me back and to you for supporting live theatre. You're the best. (A proud member of Actor's Equity Association since 1983.)
