The Cast of A NICE FAMILY CHRISTMAS

Erik Poger Abrahamsen

Erik Poger Abrahamsen is thrilled to make his Stage Door Debut in A Nice Family Christmas. Other credits include Titanic, Miss Saigon, Grease, Macbeth (Serenbe), The Giver, Miss Nelson is Missing, and Jungle Book (Georgia Ensemble Theatre). Erik is represented by the incredible folks at People Store, and he sends all love to TTSO.

Larry Davis

Larry Davis is very happy to be back at Stage Door, having previously appeared here in "The Coctail Hour", "Spreading It Around", Unnecessary Fare" (Woodie Award Best Supporting Actor), "Once Upon a Mattress" and "Picnic". Other Atlanta theatres include Actor's Express, Georgia Ensemble Theatre, The Process Theatre, The Shakespeare Tavern, The Essential Theatre, The Aurora Theatre, among others. Special thanks to Robert, Ryan and this cast and crew for epitomizing "Be Best". Love to rTodd and the Madcap Villains.

Alexandra Ficken

ALEXANDRA FICKEN is thrilled to be back at Stage Door Players! She was last seen as "Winnie Tate" in Annie Get Your Gun. She is an Atlanta native and graduate of The University of Alabama with a BA in Theatre and Dance. Other Atlanta credits include: Hand to God, The Lion, Witch and The Wardrobe, In Love and Warcraft, Bike America (Alliance); Appropriate (Actor's Express); The Electric Baby (Weird Sisters Theatre Project); ThanksKilling the Musical (Dad's Garage); Grand Concourse (Horizon); fml: How Carson McCullers Saved My Life (7 Stages); Sherlock Holmes (Georgia Ensemble). Film/TV: An Actor Prepares, Candy Jar, The DUFF, Baggage, "The Originals", "Vice Principles".

Susan Shalhoub Larkin

SUSAN SHALHOUB LARKIN Susan's last show here was "Miracle on South Division Street" for which she won the Woody for Best Actress. She is currently shooting the third season of the Netflix series "Stranger Things" in the recurring role "Flo." Other credits: the world premiere of "Two Drink Minimum" at Theatrical Outfit, the Barbara Award for Best Actress in a Play as Aida, in "Over the River and Through the Woods" at Aurora. A co-founder of The Center For Puppetry Arts, she has worked at Theatre Atlanta, Georgia Ensemble, The Alliance Theatre, Art Station, Theatre in the Square, Theatre of the Stars and Theatre Emory. Other acting credits include Broadway, TV (Monk, House of Payne, Sleepy Hollow, movies (Made Up, Feed The Fish, Tyler Perry's Good Deeds and Last Vegas with Michael Douglas). She narrates audio books, is a professional voice teacher, and just became a new real grandmother to Ashton Thomas Larkin.

Jeff K Lester

Jeff K Lester is thrilled to return to the Stage Door for this hilarious production! You may have also seen him as Joe in "Hail Mary!" here at Stage Door Players, Co-Starring on Nashville and Brockmire, or in the My RODE Reel Festival film Waiting. He would like to thank God, his family, and friends for their continued support. Enjoy the show!

Dina Shadwell

Dina Shadwell is always thrilled to return to Stage Door, whether as a director (Cemetery Club, Hail Mary, Rabbit Hole, Miracle on South Division Street) or as an actor (Private Lives, Out of Order). She has worked in Atlanta and the Southeast for almost 30 years as an actor, director, producer, puppeteer, casting director, and teaching artist. She spent 13 seasons directing Jerry’s Habima Theatre, a company featuring adult actors with developmental disabilities at the Marcus Jewish Community Center of Atlanta here in Dunwoody. She is currently running a new MJCCA program for actors with disabilities. Spotlight is an on-camera acting intensive now in its third season and currently in production for a mockumentary web series, Swampy Meadows. Dina is the Communications and Development Coordinator at the Frazer Center where she gets to do all sorts of creative things in support of Frazer’s inclusion programming for adults and children with and without disabilities. Sometimes she does graphic design, sometimes she makes art, sometimes she practices the ukulele. Big thanks to Robert for continuing to invite her back to Stage Door, and big love to Frank.
