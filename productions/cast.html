The Cast of Let Nothing You Dismay

Bryan Brendle

Bryan Brendle is tickled pink to be back at Stage Door! Around town Bryan has worked at Actors Express, ART Station, Atlanta Lyric, Aurora, Georgia Ensemble, Horizon, Pinch n Ouch, and Serenbe. He received a BFA in Acting from The Florida State University. Bryan would like to thank Robert, Topher and the entire cast and crew. Special thanks to darling Alexandra. And thank you for supporting live theatre!

Amanda Cucher

Amanda Cucher is pleased as punch to return to Stage Door Players in this crazy remount, after her previous Woodie Award winning performance in Sylvia, as well as Madge Geisel in The Game’s Afoot, or Holmes for the Holidays, Dana in Beached Wails, Randi in Greetings, and Traci in Spreading it Around. Other local theatrical endeavors include Margie in Good People, Brooke Wyeth in Other Desert Cities and Sister James in Doubt at Out of Box Theater, Evelyn in Evelyn in Purgatory with Essential Theatre Company, Bernstein in November by David Mamet and Barbara Grant in Perfect Arrangement with Process Theatre Company She would like to thank Shannon and Robert, this amazing cast, and Topher.

Mark Gray

Mark is honored to be returning to Stage Door Players to play once again in the Suzi Award winning “Let Nothing You Dismay”. Winning the Best Ensemble award and then coming back with the same cast is something every actor would want. You might remember Mark from the SDP productions of It’s A Wonderful Life A Radio Play, Lend Me A Tenor and Blythe Spirit. Additional theatre credits include 1:23 for Synchronicity Theatre as well as Based on a Totally True Story, Bent, Gypsy, Bee Luther Hatchee, Doll Plays and Auntie Mame all for Actors Express. Marks television credits include Showtime’s Comedy Club Network, Days of Our Lives on NBC and One West Waikiki for ABC. Mark has also been featured in the films World Traveler, Bobby Jones - Stroke of Genius and Tyler Perry’s Meet The Browns. A big thank you to Robert, Shannon and Topher for this second helping of fun with an amazing group of artists. Saving the biggest thanks for Virgia, his wife for more than 32 years for agreeing to be a theatre widow – once again

Shelly McCook

Shelly McCook is so happy to be back with this crazy cast, Director and the rest of the Stage Door Players gang after last years’ amazing run, and previously directing Sylvia and The Odd Couple here at SDP. Shelly is has performed or directed on most of the Atlanta professional stages including Georgia Ensemble, the late Theatre in the Square, Theatrical Outfit, 7Stages, Actors Express, Aurora, Horizon and the Alliance Theatre. She is an acting coach and teacher and will be teaching comedy classes for both adults and teens at Elm Street Arts Village this summer.  

Doyle Reynolds

Doyle Reynolds is very happy to be back at Stage Door and working with this incredibly funny, talented and award-winning group of people. He was here last year in this same show and that summer’s “Sylvia” (Woodie Award & Suzi Bass Nomination). He is an Associate Artist with Actor’s Express, where he has performed in “6 Degrees of Separation,” “100 Saints You Should Know,” “Finn In The Underworld,” “Some Men” (Suzi Bass Nomination)” and “I Am My Own Wife” (Suzi Bass Award). At Synchronicity: “Anne Boleyn” and “The Vibrator Play.”  At Geva Theatre and Aurora Theatre: “Bob.” At 7 Stages: “Mr. Universe” and “The Violet Hour.” Other credits include extensive work with Dad’s Garage Theatre, Theatre in the Square, Pinch ‘n Ouch, Jewish Theatre of The South, Horizon Theatre Company and many others.  Thank you for supporting live theatre!

Gina Rickicki

Gina Rickicki has been bringing her character-based fun to Atlanta for a few years since relocating from New York, where she received her BFA in theatre from Long Island University. Her most recent work includes When Things are Lost with the Essential Theatre. She's thrilled to be back for more frozen faced fun at Stage Door Players! You may have also caught her work with Dad’s Garage, The Weird Sisters, Horizon, and Aris Theatres. Find her in the new year in Lost in the Cosmos at Theatrical Outfit. In addition to acting, she performs her writing at live literature events around the city.

Emily Sams

Emily Sams (Allie, Lizzie) is ECSTATIC to be back at Stage Door Players! Her past credits include Amelia in The Credeaux Canvas, Carly Simon in Shadows & Light, and Jackie-O in House of Yes at Out of Box Theatre. Other favorites were Linda in The Wedding Singer, Maureen in RENT, and Lucy in You're a Good Man, Charlie Brown. Thank you to the rest of the cast for being RIDICULOUSLY TALENTED, and an absolute riot to work with. And of course Shannon and Topher - thank you for the opportunity - and for asking me back!!! HUGE thanks to family, friends, and Dan for your love and encouragement. And to anyone reading this program - thank you for supporting the local arts!

Ben Silver

Ben Silver is excited to return to Stage Door and relive one of his most fond memories of 2015. He could not be more excited to be collaborating with Topher Payne on this incredible (and Suzi winning!) project. His Atlanta credits include Tuck Everlasting (Alliance Theatre) Over The Rainbow, Six Degrees of Separation (Actor's Express) Lillian Likes It, Mysterious Connections (Essential Theatre) Film/TV: "Alone With People," "Hubcap," "Your Worst Nightmare" and "Walter Frisk, Let's Go!". Thanks to his incredible parents and this unbelievably talented ensemble.
