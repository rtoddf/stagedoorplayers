James Baskin

James Baskin (Dad) is delighted to return to Stage Door Players, where he previously appeared in Sylvia and Out of Order. He has also performed with Georgia Ensemble Theatre, Theater Emory, Onstage Atlanta, and the Essential Theatre. Many thanks to Robert—and to each of you—for the chance to work/play here again!

Rial Ellsworth

Rial Ellsworth (Jerry) is happiest whenever he gets a chance to come play on stage at Stage Door Players. He has been the resident Sound Designer here for the past five years but acting has always been his number one passion. Some of his past SDP favorites include Harry Pepper in Barefoot in the Park, Gus Klingman in Southern Comfort, Speed in The Odd Couple, and Fred Cromwell in Out of Order. But it all started back in 2007 with Professor Lyman in Bus Stop. Although he does perform at other theatres around town, this is Rial’s theatre home. He hopes you will make it yours too! Rial would like to thank Robert for his talent, vision and support, this wonderfully funny and talented cast for their generosity and friendship, and to the design team and crew that make this all run smoothly and look easy. But most of all, he thanks his long-suffering wife Kathy who continues to put up with his theatrical shenanigans after all these years!

Alexandra Ficken

Alexandra Ficken (Jill): is so excited to be back at Stage Door for another family reunion! Born and raised in Atlanta, she is also a graduate of the University of Alabama with a BA in musical theatre and dance. Other Atlanta credits include: Small Mouth Sounds, Goodnight Tyler, Hand to God, The Lion, The Witch, and The Wardrobe, In Love and Warcraft (Alliance), Appropriate (Actor's Express), A Nice Family Christmas, Annie Get Your Gun (Stage Door Players), The Electric Baby, Pretty Girl Basket Toss (Weird Sisters Theatre Project), ThanksKilling the Musical (Dad's Garage), Grand Concourse (Horizon), fml: How Carson McCullers Saved My Life (7 Stages), Sherlock Holmes (Georgia Ensemble). Thankful for this cast, crew, and Robert!

Dina Shadwell

Dina Shadwell is thrilled to be reunited with her Lundeen family after last year’s A Nice Family Christmas, and she always loves reuniting with her Stage Door family. Perhaps you’ve seen her other work here as an actor (Private Lives, Out of Order) and director (Cemetery Club, Hail Mary, Rabbit Hole, Miracle on South Division Street). She has worked in Atlanta and the Southeast for 30 years as an actor, director, producer, puppeteer, casting director, and teaching artist. She spent 13 seasons directing Jerry’s Habima Theatre, a company featuring adult actors with developmental disabilities at the Marcus Jewish Community Center of Atlanta here in Dunwoody. Also at the MJCCA she helped create and ran a 3-year on-camera acting intensive program for actors with disabilities, the Spotlight Company, for which she helped produce a mockumentary web series, Swampy Meadows, now on YouTube. Dina is the Communications and Development Coordinator at the Frazer Center where she gets to do all sorts of creative things in support of Frazer’s inclusion programming for adults and children with and without disabilities. Sometimes she does graphic design, sometimes she makes art, always she loves her partner Frank. Big thanks to Robert for continuing to invite her back to Stage Door.

Madison Welch

Madison Welch is happy to return to the Stagedoor Players family again for the holiday season. Madison is an ATL transplant from Kansas City and a proud graduate of Stephens College.  Recent credits include: Baby Shower for the Anti-Christ (Essential Theatre), Sleepy Hollow, Titanic: The Musical(Serenbe Playhouse), Ever After, Winnie the Pooh (Understudy-Alliance Theatre).
