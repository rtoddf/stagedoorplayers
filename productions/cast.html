The Cast of Picnic

Brandon Lee Browning

Brandon Lee Browning is incredibly excited to be making his Stage Door Players debut in Mass Appeal. As a 2013 graduate from High Point University with a focus in performance theatre and religion, he has thoroughly enjoyed exploring this subject matter. After relocating to Atlanta from North Carolina, as a SAG-AFTRA Eligible actor he has been pursuing theatre, film, and television in the southeast while continually keeping up his training in acting at Drama Inc. and improv at Dad's Garage. He also has an interest for writing new works and creating devised theatre pieces. Favorite past theatre performances include: The Laramie Project (Independent Artists' Playhouse), Love/Sick (Aurora Theatre AppCo Alumni Series), Brighton Beach Memoirs (Act3 Productions), The Laramie Project and Les Liaisons Dangereuses (High Point University). Brandon would like to thank everyone for being present and exploring challenging topics through this production. He would also like to thank his friends and family for their continual support of his journey.

Mark Gray

Mark Gray is honored to be returning to Stage Door Players. Mark was last at SDP in the second helping of the Suzi Award winning Let Nothing You Dismay. You might remember Mark from other SDP productions of It’s A Wonderful Life A Radio Play, Lend Me A Tenor and Blithe Spirit. Additional theatre credits include 1:23 for Synchronicity Theatre as well as Based on a Totally True Story, Bent, Gypsy, Bee Luther Hatchee, Doll Plays and Auntie Mame all for Actors Express. Mark’s television credits include Showtime’s Comedy Club Network, Days of Our Lives on NBC and One West Waikiki for ABC. Mark has also been featured in the films World Traveler, Bobby Jones - Stroke of Genius and Tyler Perry’s Meet The Browns. Thanks so much to Jimmy for his insightful direction and pushing us to be our best. A big thank you to Robert for all that he has done for SDP and bringing quality theatre to Dunwoody. Saving the biggest thanks for Virgia, his wife for more than 34 years for agreeing to be a theatre widow – once again
