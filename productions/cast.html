The Cast of The Mystery of Edwin Drood

Charlie Bradshaw

Charlie Bradshaw (Chairman/Mayor Thomas Sapsea) is thrilled to be returning to Stage Door Players where he was last seen as The Bellhop in Lend Me a Tenor. Selected musical theatre credits include Bye Bye Birdie (Harry McAfee) and Company (Harry) at Stage Door Players; Peter Pan (Smee), Red, Hot, and Cole (Monty Wooley), Man of La Mancha (The Barber), and Brigadoon (Andrew MacLaren) at Atlanta Lyric Theatre; You’re a Good Man, Charlie Brown (Charlie Brown) and Godspell (John the Baptist / Judas) at Button Theatre; Ragtime (Younger Brother) at Onstage Atlanta. Other play credits include The Woman in Black (The Actor) at Rosewater Theatre; The Three Musketeers (Aramis) at Turning Point Theater. Selected opera and operetta credits include Susannah (Sam Polk) with Capitol City Opera; The Mikado and The Pirates of Penzance with the Atlanta Lyric Theatre.  Selected concerts include Love is Rare, Other People’s Lives, An Evening with Friends, and the annual Christmas Ceildh. Charlie wishes to thank his family, friends, and especially The Colonel for their continued support.

Sarah Cave

Sarah Cave(Angela Prysock/Princess Puffer) is elated to combine her love of Dickens AND show tunes in her Stage Door debut. Sarah has performed with the Alliance Theatre, Horizon Theatre, the Essential Theatre, Actors Express, GA Ensemble Theatre, Synchronicity Performance Group, Theatrical Outfit, and the Shakespeare Tavern, among others. By day, she’s a development consultant with Ford & Associates and the mother of two wonderful children. "Working with Robert on this show is the perfect way to celebrate our 20 years of friendship!"

Sarah Cave

Sam Costantino (Master Nick Cricket/Deputy) is a rising sophomore at Marist School. He is thrilled to be back at Stage Door Players after portraying Billy in last year’s run of On Golden Pond. Sam performed in Into the Woods (Jack), Honk (Ugly), and In a Grove at Marist this past school year. Recent professional roles include Samuel in The Velveteen Rabbit (Serenbe Playhouse), Lyncoya in Blood, Bloody Andrew Jackson (Actor’s Express), Billy in Assassins (Fabrefaction Theatre Company), and Charlie in The Best Christmas Pageant Ever (Synchronicity).

Jessica de Maria

Jessica de Maria (Janet Conover/Helena Landless) is terribly excited to make her Stage Door debut. Jessica recently appeared as the Narrator in Murder Ballad at Actor's Express. Other credits include Damn Yankees (Atlanta Lyric Theatre), The Grimm Lives of the Inbetweens (Alliance Theatre), and Mary Poppins, Les Miserables (2013/2015), Petite Rouge: A Cajun Red Riding Hood (2012/2013), Big River in Concert (Aurora Theatre). Jessica has also appeared with Georgia Ensemble Theatre, Synchronicity Theatre and Pinch N' Ouch Theatre. Jessica will be appearing at The New York Musical Theatre Festival in a show she co-wrote with fellow Atlanta performer, Jeremiah Parker Hobbs, The Last Time We Were Here this July. Fondest thanks to Robert for this great show, his kindness, generosity and beautiful spirit.

Al Dollar

Al Dollar (Nick Cricker / Durdles) Highlight performances include Theater of the Stars touring productions of Hello, Dolly! and The Music Man, a European tour aboard the original Love Boat, Walt Disney World productions of Diamond Horseshoe, Beauty and the Beast, Kids of the Kingdom, and The Magical World of Barbie, The Mickey Mouse Club (with Britney, Justin, and Christina), The Shoji Tabuchi Theatre and Melinda, The First Lady of Magic. Recently, Because It’s Christmas at Six Flags Over Georgia, Atlanta Lyric Theatre productions of Annie, Anything Goes! and The Music Man, Willy Wonka with Fabrefaction Theatre, Bye, Bye Birdie! with Stage Door Players, The Secret Garden, Parade, Medea, Arcadia, and Jekyll and Hyde with Theatre Arts Guild at Georgia Perimeter College. Offstage: Disney's Reading Together with Belle Tour, St. Jude Children’s Research Hospital 40th Anniversary Shower of Stars, Andre Agassi’s Grand Slam for Children, Love Rocks (Honoring Bono of U2), IMPACT at Ford Field, and multiple televised award shows including The Academy Awards, Primetime Emmy Awards, ESPY Awards, Grammy Awards and MTV Video and Movie Awards.

Jordan Dell Harris

Jordan Dell Harris (Victor Grinstead/Neville Landless) is an Atlanta-based actor and visual artist, and he couldn't be happier to be back at Stage Door. He was last seen here as Sir Harry in Once Upon a Mattress. Other Atlanta credits include Six Degrees of Separation and Spring Awakening at Actor's Express, The Grimm Lives of the Inbetweens at the Alliance Theatre, West Side Story at Fabrefaction, Damn Yankees at the Atlanta Lyric Theatre, and Hidden Man and Curious Encounters II at 7 Stages. Many thanks to Robert and the Stage Door family for inviting me back to play. 

Daniel Hilton

Daniel Hilton (Clive Paget/John Jasper) is very excited to be playing a role he has had his heart set on since he was 17, when he could regularly be found obsessively listening to the original cast recording of The Mystery of Edwin Drood on repeat; sitting on the floor by the boom box, following along with the lyrics and synopsis. A graduate of Kennesaw State University’s Department of Theatre and Performance Studies, Daniel has worked variously as an actor, composer, and musical director at Theatrical Outfit, Aurora Theatre, Synchronicity, Georgia Ensemble Theatre, and, of course, Stage Door Players. He thinks singing, dancing, and being a schizophrenic opium addict is pretty much the best fun to be had. Love and kisses to his sons Eden and Jude (who knows all the words to Both Sides of the Coin!).

Robert Lee Hindsman

Robert Lee Hindsman (Montague Pruitt/Throttle) is very excited to make his Stage Door Players debut with The Mystery of Edwin Drood.  He just recently completed an acting apprenticeship with Serenbe Playhouse after graduating with a B.A. in Theatre from Birmingham Southern College.  He wants to thank Robert and the Stage Door Players team for the opportunity, his parents for all of their continued love and support over the years, and a special thanks to you guys for supporting live theatre.

Stuart W. Schleuse

Stuart W. Schleuse (Cedric Moncrieffe/Rev. Crisparkle) is most excited to be returning to Stage Door Players after a 13 year absence. He was Last seen at SDP as the Emcee in Bells Are Ringing in 2002. More recently, Stuart was seen understudying the role of Angela Arden in Process Theatre’s runaway camp hit, Die, Mommie Die!. Prior to that, he appeared as the Bishop of Digne in The Aurora Theatre’s remount of their award-winning production of Les Misérables.  Other appearances around Atlanta include Mary Poppins and the 2013 production of Les Misérables (also at The Aurora), Jacob Marley’s Christmas Carol (Out of Box Theatre), Copenhagen (Academy Theater), See What I Wanna See (Actor’s Express, Suzi Award for Best Ensemble–Musical), Parade (Theater Arts Guild), and Greater Tuna (OnStage Atlanta), among others. Stuart is also a member of the chorus of the Atlanta Opera and a Speech Pathologist at Emory University Hospital. Stuart would like to dedicate his performance to the memory of his long-time mentor and dear friend, Jo Howarth.
