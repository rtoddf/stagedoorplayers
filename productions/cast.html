The Cast of Southern Comforts

Rial Ellsworth

Rial is honored to be a part of this beautiful heartwarming story. He has been an active actor in Atlanta’s theater community for many years and has performed at many of the city’s best known theatrical venues, as well as many of its lesser known ones as well. Favorite roles on the dramatic side include King Lear, Lyman Wyeth (Other Desert Cities), Big Daddy (Cat on a Hot Tin Roof), Frank Strang (Equus) and Rabbi Berger (Pangs of the Messiah). Favorite comedy roles include Coach Fred DeSalvo (Topher Payne’s Evelyn in Purgatory), Speed (The Odd Couple), Fred Cromwell (Out of Order) and Pearl Burrus (Greater Tuna, Tuna Christmas & Heart of Tuna). He wishes to thank the lovely KK ladies – Director Kate Donadio & scene partner Karen Howell - for their faith, guidance and support in this production. Also big thanks to Artistic Director Robert Egizio for providing the kind of theater that we can all be so proud to be a part of. Rial can never have a bio that doesn’t thank his ever loving and ever patient wife Kathy, because, well, she’s ever loving and ever patient, and she makes it possible for him to do this. Finally huge thanks to you for support live theatre in Atlanta. Without you, all we’d ever do is rehearse! Enjoy the show!

Karen Howell

Karen Howell is very happy to be appearing in her first show with Stage Door Players. She began her Atlanta theatre career back in the late 70's with Theatre Emory. Karen was a member of the first Acting Intern Company at the Alliance Theatre. She has performed in many of Atlanta's professional theatres, notably the late, great Theatre in the Square, where for 20 seasons she appeared in all the Sanders Family productions. Karen has also performed regionally with Riverside Theatre (FL), American Stage (NH), Gretna Playhouse (PA), Blowing Rock Stage, Charlotte Rep, and several summers at Flat Rock Playhouse in NC. She was last seen locally in Georgia Ensemble's "On Slight Hitch". Karen is a two time Suzi Bass Award winner, and recipient of the 2005 "Spirit of Suzi" award for service to the Atlanta theatre community. She has been a proud member of Actors" Equity since 1984.
