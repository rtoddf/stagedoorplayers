The Cast of Annie Get Your Gun

Sam Costantino

Sam Costantino is extremely excited to return to Stage Door Players for Annie Get Your Gun! His other Stage Door appearances include On Golden Pond (Billy) and The Mystery of Edwin Drood (Nick Cricker, Jr.). Sam has performed in other professional productions in Atlanta: A Christmas Story (Georgia Shakespeare), Assassins (Fabrefaction), Bloody Bloody Andrew Jackson (Actor's Express) and The Velveteen Rabbit (Serenbe Playhouse). Other favorite roles in school productions are Oliver! (Oliver, St. Thomas More); Into the Woods (Jack), Lucky Stiff (Harry Witherspoon) and Shrek The Musical (Pinocchio), performed at Marist School. Sam thanks Robert Egizio for this opportunity.

George Deavours

George Deavours is delighted to be joining the amazing cast of Annie Get Your Gun, and reuniting with friends from Kiss Me, Kate! Recent stage appearances include John Adams in 1776 at the Legacy Theatre, and Maurice in Beauty and the Beast at the Atlanta Lyric Theatre. A veteran of over 75 shows, some favorite roles include: Gus, the Theater Cat in CATS, Amos Hart in Chicago, Albin/Zaza in la Cage aux Folles, Major-General Stanley in The Pirates of Penzance, Beadle Bamford in Sweeney Todd, Baldy in A Man of No Importance, Mr. Weber/ Cigar in Gypsy. Immediately after Annie Get your Gun, George will be appearing as Alfred Doolittle in My Fair Lady at the Atlanta Lyric Theatre. The wig designer for over 250 shows, some credits include Kiss Me, Kate!, The Producers, Dreamgirls, High Society, Once Upon a Mattress, Guys and Dolls, Ragtime, Legally Blonde, Gypsy, Beehive, Thoroughly Modern Millie, White Christmas, and The Mikado. A professional hairstylist, he has a studio at French Door Salon in Duluth.

Jeremy Wood

Jeremy Wood is an Atlanta-based actor with a B.F.A. in Music and Theatre from the former Shorter College.  Jeremy has toured nationally and internationally with the national tour of Grease as well as the first national tour of Ring of Fire.  Jeremy was recognized by the Kennedy Center in 2003 for his work as a fight choreographer with the American College Theatre Festival's award-winning production of Jekyll and Hyde and is also the recipient of 2 New Hampshire Theatre Awards. In 2010 he was included in Creative Loafing's top 10 performances in Atlanta and was awarded Best Featured Actor in a Musical in the 2011 Suzi Bass Awards for his role as “Cosmo Brown” in Aurora Theatre's Singin' in the Rain. Jeremy is excited and grateful to return to Stage Door Players for this production of Annie Get Your Gun.
