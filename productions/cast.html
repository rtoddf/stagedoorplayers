ROBERT EGIZIO

ROBERT EGIZIO now at the start of his fourteenth season as Producing Artistic Director of Stage Door Players, Robert would like to take this opportunity to thank the Staff, Board of Directors, Season Subscribers, community partners, volunteers, artists, audience members, and sponsors for allowing him to do what he loves so very much. As a director and choreographer, Robert has been involved with numerous theater companies in the Atlanta area. He served as choreographer in residence for Actors Express, and has worked with the Atlanta Opera, ART Station, the Aurora Theatre, Gainesville Theater Alliance, Georgia Perimeter College, Georgia Ensemble Theatre, The Center Theatre, and Onstage Atlanta. Some of his favorite Stage Door productions he has directed include;  Annie Get Your Gun, Perfect Wedding, Hot ‘n Cole, Leading Ladies, Private Lives, Pippin, Boeing Boeing, The Fourposter, Unnecessary Farce, High Society, Once Upon a Mattress, Lend Me a Tenor, and The Mystery of Edwin Drood. As an actor, his credits include performances locally, nationally and on tour. In the Atlanta area, he has appeared at Georgia Ensemble Theatre, Aurora Theater, Actors Express, ART Station, Jewish Theatre of the South, and right here at Stage Door Players. Robert also wishes to send great thanks to Kate and this amazing cast of actors, the hardest working and incredible production team who make the magic happen and of course to Chuck, who supports him like no other. And finally a special thanks to all who support our efforts to bring you the best in professional live theater.

CHARLES GREEN

CHARLES GREEN last appeared at Stage Door Players in 2005 as Sidney Bruhl in Deathtrap (Woodie Award Nominee Best Performance by a Lead Actor).  His 2017 onstage roles are Reverend Parris in The Crucible (Actor’s Express) and Martini in One Flew Over the Cuckoo’s Nest (Renaissance Theatre Project).  He is the recipient of a Metropolitan Atlanta Theatre Award for his performance in Process Theatre’s Perfect Arrangement. His recent directorial efforts are Bouncers (Audience Award 2016 Atlanta Fringe Festival), and Why Hanna’s Skirt Won’t Stay Down (Get Scene Studios). 2017 screen credits: TV’s  Outsiders (WGN), Turn: Washington’s Spies (AMC), and MacGyver (CBS); and the films Mark Felt and Thor: Ragnarok. Charles is represented by Privilege Talent.

DAVIN ALLEN GRINDSTAFF

DAVIN ALLEN GRINDSTAFF is excited to be returning to Stage Door Players. Davin has also worked at Out Front Theatre Company, Georgia Ensemble Theatre, Gypsy Theatre, Essential Theatre, Fabrefaction Theatre Conservatory, Out of Box Theatre, and Onstage Atlanta, among others. He won the 2014 M.A.T. Award for Best Actor in a Play (Allan Felix, Play It Again, Sam). When not on stage, Davin lives a double-life as a professor of Speech Communication at Georgia State University and a professional figure skater. Look for the pilot episode of his new comedy series, Walter Frisk Lets Go on YouTube. He thanks his parents for teaching him the value of doing what you love

KRISTIN MARKITON

KRISTIN MARKITON is delighted to be making her debut appearance here at Stage Door Players, and especially thrilled to be sharing the stage once again with her old pal, Robert Egezio after having played his love interest in Actor’s Express’ production of Wonderful Town SO many years ago. She most recently appeared as Francesca Johnson in Bridges of Madison County and Step-Mother/Witch understudy in Into the Woods, both at Aurora Theatre. Other notable Atlanta stage credits include Spitfire Grill (The Horizon Theater), Master Class (Theatre in the Square), Slam (Jomandi Productions), Della’s Diner (The Canton Theatre), as well as A Little Night Music (Desiree), Kismet (Lalume) and The Merry Widow (ZoZo), all of which were produced at Savoyards Light Opera (now Atlanta Lyric Theater). Kristin is also a performing songwriter who has released two studio albums: her debut solo album, Wandering Moon (https://store.cdbaby.com/cd/markiton) and Howlin’, a collaboration with the Atlanta-based band, Delta Moon (https://store.cdbaby.com/cd/deltamoon3). You can also catch her playing down at Blind Wilie’s blues club about once a month with her old-school blues/soul band, Kristin & the Honey Badgers.

CHAD MARTIN

CHAD MARTIN is thrilled to be returning to Stage Door Players after appearing in past productions of Greetings and Visiting Mr. Green. He has also performed at other theatres in the community including Aurora Theatre, Actor’s Express, Georgia Ensemble Theatre, Horizon Theatre, Synchronicity Theatre, and the former Theatre in the Square. Big thanks to Robert, Kate, and the whole Stage Door crew for a killer experience. Big love to wife and partner in crime Kelly and our doodle girl Daisy. Enjoy the show and thank you for supporting the arts!

DOYLE REYNOLDS

DOYLE REYNOLDS is elated to be back at Stage Door where he’s appeared in both productions of “Let Nothing You Dismay” (Suzi Award Best Ensemble) and in “Sylvia” (Woodie Award & Suzi Nomination). He is an Associate Artist with Actor’s Express, where he has performed in “6 Degrees of Separation,” “100 Saints You Should Know,” “Finn In The Underworld,” “Some Men” (Suzi Nomination)” and “I Am My Own Wife” (Suzi Award). At Synchronicity: “Anne Boleyn” and “The Vibrator Play.”  At Geva Theatre and Aurora Theatre: “Bob.” At 7 Stages: “Mr. Universe” and “The Violet Hour.” Other credits include extensive work with Dad’s Garage Theatre, Theatre in the Square, Pinch ‘n Ouch, Jewish Theatre of The South, Horizon Theatre Company and many others.  Thank you for supporting live theatre!
