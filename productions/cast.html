The Cast of THE GLASS MENAGERIE

KATIE CAUSEY

KATIE CAUSEY is thrilled to perform with Stage Door Players! Most recently Katie was onstage at Horizon Theatre Co. in Sarah DeLappe's play, The Wolves. You can also find Katie hosting and performing stand-up comedy at the Aurora Theatre and in a variety of random commercials playing in the southeast. When she's not acting, Katie works as an Education Artist with The Alliance Theatre and The Company Acting Studio and as an On-Set Coach for children. Thank you for supporting live theatre!  Much love to Jon!

JONATHAN HORNE

JONATHAN HORNE is thrilled to do his first Williams and have his Stage Door Players debut at the same time! Previous ATL credits include Night Must Fall and Elephant Man* (GET) Miss Bennet: Christmas at Pemberley (TO) Hamlet, Coriolanus, and Hal*/Henry V (ASC), and The Crucible* (AE). *Three time Suzi Bass nominee, winner for The Crucible. Film/TV credits include A (legally required non-specified) upcoming Disney + show, “MacGyver”, “The Originals”, “Devious Maids”, and the feature films No Sin Unpunished and Student Teacher. Thanks to Topher for all your work, soul, and leadership. Thank you to my amazing agents at Privilege talent for letting me take time to do theatre. Love to my family, and "alot" of love to Laura. Soli Deo Gloria. Jonathanhorneactor.com

SHELLY MCCOOK

SHELLY MCCOOK has been a part of the Atlanta professional theatre community since graduating with a theatre degree from the University of Alabama, oh so many years ago. She has had the privilege of acting or directing on most of Atlanta’s stages including  Alliance, Horizon, Aurora, Theatrical Outfit, 7 Stages and Georgia Ensemble Theatre. She is thrilled to return to Stage Door. Previous credits include directing Sylvia, The Odd Couple and Christmas at Sweet Apple. She also played multiple characters in the two productions of Topher Payne’s Let Nothing You Dismay. She has served on the board of the Jo Howarth Noonan Foundation which produces the annual MoJo Fest and is a full-time theatre educator with Fulton County Schools. Shelly owes a debt of gratitude to her friends and family who helped to make this opportunity  a reality, especially Lisa, Mom, Richard and Cheri.

BENJAMIN STRICKLAND

BENJAMIN STRICKLAND, from Auburn AL, is a graduate of Auburn University and works on and behind the stages around Atlanta. Previous credits include: The Hunchback of Notre-Dame, Christmas Canteen 2017, Mamma Mia, Newsies! (Aurora Theatre), Nick's Flamingo Grill (U/s, Alliance Theatre), and a return to his alma mater to perform in Big Fish (Auburn University). Off stage, Ben works as an electrician in the Atlanta theatre community, and is never far from his computer if he can help it!
