The Cast of AIN'T MISBEHAVIN'

PATTY GUENTHNER

PATTY GUENTHNER is thrilled to be in this empowering production with these wonderful women! The last time she was on stage at SDP was when she stepped in for an ill Narrator in Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat, so it has been a while. She has been performing in Atlanta area theater for over 30 years (gulp!) Some favorite roles include Mrs. Graves In Enchanted April at OnStage Atlanta and as Molina's mother in Kiss of the Spider Woman at Actor's Express. More recently, Patty has turned to directing The More Players at St. Thomas More in Decatur. By day, she 'performs' HIV research at the CDC.

NANCY LOWERY POWELL

NANCY LOWERY POWELL is excited to be making her debut with Stage Door Players. She has most recently performed at Lionheart Theater (Pump Boys and Dinettes), Staged Right Theater (Matt and Ben), and Live Arts Theater (Second Samuel). She has also performed with On Stage Atlanta, The Essential Theater, The Alliance Theater, New Depot Players, and Winder Barrow Community Theater. Nancy works as an instructional coach, and she is grateful to all of her family and friends who support her when she steps out on a stage.

SUZANNE ROUSH

SUZANNE ROUSH is so happy to be sipping libations with such lively ladies on such a lovely veranda!  Stage Door audiences may remember her from A Red Plaid Shirt last season. Suzanne was last seen in Essential Theatre's Summer Play Festival and is very grateful to Robert and to Cathe for this opportunity with Stage Door. 

KAREN WHITAKER

KAREN WHITAKER has performed in theatre for over 48 years. In Atlanta she has worked with Aurora Theatre, Art Station, OnStage Atlanta, Theatre Emory, and Process Theatre, among others. Previous SDP roles include Lucille in The Cemetery Club, Julia in Lend Me a Tenor (Suzi Award nominee), Charlotte Hay in Moon Over Buffalo, and Grace in Bus Stop (Woodie Award). Other favorites include Lexie in Dixie Swim Club, Martha in Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?, Doris in Same Time, Next Year, Kitty in Perfect Arrangement (MAT Award) and Mame in Mame. Karen was also honored to play Tallulah Bankhead at the Spoleto Festival in Charleston, SC. Over her 33 years in Atlanta theatre, other favorite credits include Parallel Lives – The Kathy and Mo Show (a two-woman show in which she played 17 different characters), Della's Diner – Blue Plate Special (Della), Best Little Whorehouse in Texas (Miss Mona), I Do! I Do! (Agnes), A Little Night Music (Desiree), and Mating Habits of the Urban Mammal (Martha). By day, you can find her managing projects, leading process improvement and herding cats at Georgia Transmission Corporation. Much love to my amazing “family of friends.”
