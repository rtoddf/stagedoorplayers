The Cast of Southern Comforts

James Donadio

James Donadio is happy to be back at Stage Door, but this time in front of the lights! You saw his work recently as a Director on last season's hit production of Rounding Third for which he won the Woodie Award for Best Direction of a Play. On numerous occasions he has served as Artist in Residence and Guest Artist at a number of college and universities including San Francisco State University, the University of Colorado, Wright State University, Colorado Mountain College, the University of Wisconsin, Tulane University, and Theatre Emory. He has had the opportunity to work in many other capacities across the county on a wide variety of plays, from classical to modern, from dramas to comedies, and musicals, including The Cherry Orchard, A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum, Look Back in Anger, The Foreigner, The Marowitz Hamlet, the premier of The Invisible Man, Amy's View, The Odd couple, Twentieth Century, Ethan Frome, and Deathtrap. James is married to the wonderful actress/director Shannon Eubanks, proud father of actress and director Kate Donadio, "Papa-in-Law" to the multi-talented Jonathan McQueen, Grandfather to Kate and Jonathan's beautiful boys, Theirry and Flynn.

Karen Howell

Karen Howell is very happy to be appearing in her first show with Stage Door Players. She began her Atlanta theatre career back in the late 70's with Theatre Emory. Karen was a member of the first Acting Intern Company at the Alliance Theatre. She has performed in many of Atlanta's professional theatres, notably the late, great Theatre in the Square, where for 20 seasons she appeared in all the Sanders Family productions. Karen has also performed regionally with Riverside Theatre (FL), American Stage (NH), Gretna Playhouse (PA), Blowing Rock Stage, Charlotte Rep, and several summers at Flat Rock Playhouse in NC. She was last seen locally in Georgia Ensemble's "On Slight Hitch". Karen is a two time Suzi Bass Award winner, and recipient of the 2005 "Spirit of Suzi" award for service to the Atlanta theatre community. She has been a proud member of Actors" Equity since 1984.
