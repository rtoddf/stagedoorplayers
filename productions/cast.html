The Cast of THE LAST NIGHT OF BALLYHOO

Pamela Gold

Pamela Gold* is thrilled to perform in her first production with Stage Door Players. She has appeared on Broadway in the original revival companies of How to Succeed In Business Without Really Trying, performing as Hedy La Rue opposite Matthew Broderick, and in Once Upon A Mattress with Sarah Jessica Parker. She has toured nationally with A Chorus Line (Bebe), and has performed many lead and featured roles in regional theatres across the country, including Goodspeed Opera House, Pittsburgh Public Theatre, and the Kennedy Center, as well as originating roles in the world premieres of What the World Needs Now at The Old Globe Theatre, and the pre-Broadway Disney production of Elton John and Tim Rice's Elaborate Lives: The Legend of Aida at The Alliance Theatre. Other favorite Atlanta credits include From Door to Door and Chopped Liver in Paradise at Jewish Theatre of the South, Degas' Little Dancer at The Alliance, and Singin' in the Rain (Lina Lamont) and A Chorus Line (Cassie) at Aurora Theatre. Pamela is also a member of the 2018-2019 Acting Ensemble for Working Title Playwrights. She is a Theatre Studies graduate of Yale University. Thanks to Michael, Lenore, Maya, and Nolan for their love and support, and to Mira and Robert for this opportunity to play this deliciously overprotective, domineering Jewish mother. It's a stretch.

Maggie Birgel

Maggie Birgel is thrilled to be making her Stage Door debut with Ballyhoo! Previous Atlanta theatre credits include Rudolph the Red-Nosed Reindeer (Center for Puppetry Arts), Nell Gwynn, Strait of Gibraltar, Fancy Nancy (Synchronicity), Enchanted April (Weird Sisters), Damn Yankees (Theatre Buford), Madeline's Christmas (Horizon), Calendar Girls, Romeo and Juliet, STEAM TEAM, And Then They Came For Me (Georgia Ensemble). You can see her next in Bullets Over Broadway at Georgia Ensemble Theatre in April. 

Elliott Folds

Elliott Folds is delighted to be making his debut with Stage Door Players! He is a proud graduate of Kennesaw State University and Aurora Theatre's 2016-17 Apprentice Company. Previous credits include the one-man comedy Buyer & Cellar (Out Front Theatre Company/ART Station), A Year with Frog and Toad (Synchronicity Theatre), Love's Labour's Lost (Zero Circle Theatre Company), The Bridges of Madison County (Aurora Theatre), and Damn Yankees (Theatre Buford). Huge thanks to Robert, Mira, and the whole Ballyhoo team for this opportunity, and as always, buckets of love to Mom, Dad, Ansley, Emily, and Abby, and an extra shout-out to his wonderful girlfriend Caty.

Shaun MacLean

Shaun MacLean is honored to be making his debut with Stage Door Players in such an iconic Atlanta play! Since (somehow) graduating from University of Georgia in 2015 he has been pursuing his acting career both on stage and on camera, and is a proud alumni of the ‘17-‘18 Horizon Theatre Apprentice Company. Recent credits include: A Christmas Carol with Theatre Buford, The Pillowman with Independent Artists' Playhouse, Freaky Friday at Horizon Theatre, and She Kills Monsters at Out of Box Theatre. Thank you to Robert, Mira, and everyone in the cast and crew for this incredible experience. Shaun would not have made it this far without unconditional love and support from Mom, Dad, Devin and Carleigh, all of his incredible friends, Mr. Rick Andosca, and the fine folks at People Store. Love y'all, enjoy the show!

Lucy Rose Gross

Lucy Rose Gross is excited to share her debut at Stage Door Players. After graduating with her degree in Musical Theatre from Birmingham-Southern College, she completed a year long acting internship with Actors Express in Atlanta. She is currently a teacher and administrative assistant at Bach to Rock in Alpharetta. Favorite credits include Carrie (BSC Theatre), The Harvey Milk Show, The Crucible, and Company (Actors Express). She would like to dedicate this performance to her late father, Howard Gross. 

Jared Simon

Jared Simon is thrilled to be making his Stage Door Players debut. He was last seen in the Aurora Theatre production of Driving Miss Daisy. Over the last 25 years He has appeared at many Atlanta theaters including Horizon Theatre, Theatre in the Square, Jewish Theater of the South, Actor's Express, Barking Dog Theater, Neighborhood Playhouse, and Art station. Many New York credits include the Broadway production of An Enemy of the People at Roundabout Theatre Company. He can also be seen on various TV shows including most recently “Brockmire “, “Love is_ “, and “Black  lightning”. Big love to Martha

Ann Wilson

Ann Wilson is so very happy to be back home at Stage Door Players where audiences may remember her as Ethel Banks in Barefoot in the Park, Ida in The Cemetery Club, Sister Regina in Hail Mary! (Woodie award) and Kate Mundy in Dancing at Lughnasa.  Last season, Ann was privileged to perform in two plays by Topher Payne, first as Louise Carlyle in the world premiere of Morningside at Georgia Ensemble Theatre and then as Kitty Sunderson in Perfect Arrangement at Theatrical Outfit.  Both productions were Suzi nominated for best ensemble. Ann then spent the late summer as Helen in The Electric Baby for The Weird Sisters Theatre Project. She has worked around the country in places like New York, North Carolina and Texas.  Atlanta has been home for over 30 years and Ann has performed for The Alliance, Horizon Theatre Company, Actor's Express, Essential Theatre, Center for Puppetry Arts, Gainesville Theatre Alliance and Theatre in the Square.  One of the last movies she watched with her late mother was Mr. Uhry's Driving Miss Daisy, many years ago.  Ann's work in this show is dedicated to the memory of her mama with deepest love and appreciation.
