Private Lives

A comedy of manners and marriage, ex-lovers Amanda Prynne and Elyot Chase find themselves both honeymooning with their new spouses at the same hotel in France, old resentments quickly fan the fires of an old flame. Always lively, Coward's script is filled to the brim with clever barbs and witty retorts as Amanda and Elyot bounce back and forth between a state of passionate infatuation and ferocious hostility.

January 16 through February 8, 2015
By Noel Coward

Private Lives Cast

Tess Malis Kincaid

Tess Malis Kincaid is so delighted to be doing this wonderful play here at Stage Door with such a great cast, director, and design team. She has directed two productions here in recent years, Dancing at Lughnasa and Same Time, Next Year, and now the tables are turned (cue Robert's evil grin). Tess most recently appeared in Clybourne Park at the Aurora, and Dividing the Estate at Theatrical Outfit. She has appeared on stages throughout Atlanta, as well as in San Francisco, Washington DC, and North Carolina. In addition to acting and directing projects, Tess also works as Resources Director at Georgia Ensemble Theatre in Roswell. Much love to her leading man, onstage and off, Mark, and to our beautiful daughter, Barbara Rose, for her extraordinary support and patience. Thank you for supporting the arts in Atlanta, and Happy 2015!

Private Lives Cast

Mark Kincaid

Mark is so pleased to be working with Robert Egizio and the wonderful people of Stage Door Players. Locally his work has been seen on the stages of The Alliance Theatre: The Grapes of Wrath, Zorro, God's Man In Texas, The Miracle Worker, Southern Cross, Carapace and Medea. The Georgia Shakespeare Festival: Richard II, Hamlet, Tartuffe, Twelfth Night, A Midsummer Nights Dream and A Christmas Story.

For eight seasons, Mr. Kincaid was a company member at The North Carolina Shakespeare Festival where he played a variety of roles including Prospero, Macbeth, and Cyrano De Bergerac and has twice toured the nation with The Acting Company. He is very happily married to the extraordinarily beautiful and gifted actress Tess Malis Kincaid, and is the very proud father of the remarkable Barbara Rose Kincaid.

Rachel Garner

Rachel is thrilled to finally be on the Stage Door Players stage, after appearing as in last season's joint SDP/MJCCA production of Letters to Sala. Most recently, she appeared as Mrs. Kendal in The Elephant Man at Georgia Ensemble Theatre. Other stage credits include Jordan Baker in The Great Gatsby at GET, Mary in Lark Eden and Mother Type in Neighborhood 3: Requisition of Doom at Aurora Theatre, Sy in Fair Use at Actor's Express and Susan Smith in 1:23 at Synchronicity Theatre. Many thanks and much love to Robert, and of course to Angela.
