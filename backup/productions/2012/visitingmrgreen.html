Visiting Mr. Green

January 25 through February 17, 2013

Mr. Green wanders into New York traffic and is almost hit by a car driven by Ross Gardiner. When the young man is given community service to help the recent widower we find ourselves at the start of a comedy/drama about two men who at first can't stand to be in the same room together. But as they get to know each other, they find some shaky common ground, and help to heal the old wounds they've both been hiding.

by Jeff Baron

Performance times

Thursday, Friday, and Saturday @ 8:00pm, Sunday Matinees @ 2:30pm

Tickets can also be purchased online from: TIX.com

Meet the Visiting Mr. Green Cast

Theo Harness (Mr. Green)

Theo appears for the first time on the Stage Door boards. He is an original member of the Actor's Express, where he designed and performed for years, and has been seen at most other theatres in the Atlanta area-most recently in JibJam's production of Brian Friel's Faith Healer for which he won a Suzi nomination for Best Featured Actor- Play.

He has been to Portland Center Stage in Craig Lucas's This Thing of Darkness, and for Itimar Moses' Outrage and Chekov's The Seagull. Other favorite roles have been in Shaw's Heartbreak House at the Alliance Theatre (where he worked with Courtney Loner and Suzi Bass), Long Day's Journey Into Night for Soulstice Rep, and at Actor's Express: Jeffrey and Oklahoma (with Robert Egizio), Twelfth Night, The Cherry Orchard, Cloud Nine and so many others. Theo is a member of Actor's Equity Association.

Meet the Visiting Mr. Green Cast

Chad Martin (Ross Gardiner)

is happy to be back at Stage Door after appearing as Andy in last season's Christmas show Greetings. Some of his favorite Atlanta credits include: Brilliant Traces at Synchronicity, Inherit the Wind at GET, Dracula at Aurora Theatre, and The Little Dog Laughed at Theatre in the Square.
