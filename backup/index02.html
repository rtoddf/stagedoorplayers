Lend Me a Tenor

May 18 through June 10, 2012
(Performances Friday and Saturday evenings and Sunday afternoons)

The time is September, 1934. The place is the Cleveland Grand Opera Company. World famous tenor Tito Morelli is to perform Otello, his greatest role, at the gala season opener. Through a hilarious series of mishaps and a double dose of tranquilizers, he passes out and is no where to be found. Is Il Stupendo dead? Who will go in his place? Considered by some to be one of the greatest farces ever written, this one will have you rolling in the aisles.

by Ken Ludwig

Meet the Cast of Lend Me a Tenor

Kelly Chapin Schmidt

Kelly Chapin Schmidt (Maggie) is delighted to be returning to Stage Door after appearing in last season's A Grand Night for Singing and receiving the Shayne Kohout Newcomer Award at the 2011 Woody's. Atlanta credits: Grease (Frenchy) & The Sound of Music (Sister Margaretta) Atlanta Lyric Theatre, A Chorus Line (Maggie) Aurora Theatre, and The 25th Annual Putnam County Spelling Bee (Schwartzy) Georgia Ensemble Theatre.

Other Regional credits: West Side Story (Maria), Fiddler on the Roof (Fruma Sarah), and Nunsense (Sister Mary Leo). BFA Music Theatre from Elon University.

Eliana Marianes

Eliana Marianes (Maria Terelli) studied Theater Studies and English at Emory University, and is thrilled to be making her Stage Door debut. She has performed with the Alliance Theater, Georgia Shakespeare Festival, Synchronicity Theatre, Aurora Theater, Out of Hand Theater, the Legacy Theatre, the Center for Puppetry Arts, Georgia Ensemble Theater, Theater Emory, Emory Dance Company, Academy Theatre, Theatre Decatur, and Theatre du Reve, both in Atlanta and in Limoges, France. She has loved every moment of her theater career so far, from creation to final curtain, and she is very happy to be here!

Theater Classes For Children

ONCE UPON A BALLET comes to STAGE DOOR PLAYERS.

Once Upon a Ballet & Musical Theater™ offers unique dance and performing arts programs for children ages 2-14. From our Creative division to our Upper school classes, our mission is to foster the creative spirit in your child, by providing sound technical training in a nurturing environment. Handmade costumes, delicate props and timeless stories are an essential part of our Creative curriculum, while Classic ballets and Broadway musicals are the focus of our Upper school curriculum.

What is your child's passion? Ballet! Tap! Acting! Singing! Once Upon a Ballet & Musical Theater™ has it all. Contact Once Upon a Ballet & Musical Theater™ at onceuponaballetatl@gmail.com and ask about special Introductory Offers for Stage Door Players patrons!!

Meet the Cast of Lend Me a Tenor

John Markowski

John Markowski could not be happier to be returning to the Stage Door stage! John was last seen at SDP in the regional premiere of Unnecessary Farce. Other Stage Door credits include Moon Over Buffalo (Paul) and the regional premiere of Leading Ladies (Butch Meyers).

Other Atlanta credits: Singin' In the Rain, Damn Yankees, The Dinosaur Musical at the Aurora Theatre, A Funny Thing Happened On The Way To The Forum, Beauty and The Beast, Anything Goes, Peter Pan at the Atlanta Lyric Theatre, Torch Song Trilogy Process Theatre Company, Fuddy Meers, The Wild Party, Urinetown The Musical, among others at Onstage Atlanta. John holds a BA in Theatre: Performance from the College of Charleston and is currently a full time employee with Kaiser Permanente's Educational Theatre Programs of Georgia.

Meet the Cast of Lend Me a Tenor

Larry Ruth

Larry Ruth has directed two productions for Stage Door Players (BREAKING LEGS and GO BACK FOR MURDER), but this marks his debut as an actor for this wonderful theatre. Roles Larry has performed include Jack in BRIGHTON BEACH MEMOIRS (also directed by Robert Egizio) at Center Theatre, Dracula, Ron in MARRIED ALIVE at ART Station, and many musical theatre roles. His directorial credits include productions of ENCHANTED APRIL, A MIDSUMMER NIGHT'S DREAM, GRAPES OF WRATH, among many others. Recently he has had the opportunity to direct staged readings for Working Title Playwrights where he gets to work with playwrights in developing their works. Larry is so happy to be working with Robert again and with this dynamic company and great cast.
