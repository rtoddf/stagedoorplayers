Directions to Stage Door Players

Stage Door Players is located at 5339 Chamblee Dunwoody Road approx 1 block South of Mt Vernon Rd and Chamblee Dunwoody Rd in the North Dekalb Cultural Arts Center.

Coming from West

  • - Take I-285 East to Exit 29 - Ashford Dunwoody Rd
  • - Turn Left onto Ashford Dunwoody and continue past Perimeter Mall
  • - Turn Right at Ashford Center Parkway and continue to the next light
  • - Turn Left onto Chamblee Dunwoody Road
  • - Take the 2nd driveway on your Right into the North Dekalb Cultural Center

Coming from East

  • - Take I-285 West to Exit 29 - Ashford Dunwoody Rd
  • - Turn Right onto Ashford Dunwoody and continue past Perimeter Mall
  • - Take a Right at Ashford Center Parkway and continue to the next light. Turn Left onto Chamblee Dunwoody Road
  • - Take the 2nd driveway on your Right into the North Dekalb Cultural Center

Coming from South

  • - Head North on 400 to Exit 5a (Dunwoody)
  • - Merge onto Abernathy Rd and turn left at Mt Vernon Rd
  • - Continue a few miles to Chamblee Dunwoody Road and turn right
  • - We're on the left in the North Dekalb Cultural Center

Coming from North

  • - Head South on 400 to Exit 6 (Northridge Rd)
  • - Turn Right off the exit (back over Rt 400) and take a right at the 4-way stop sign onto Roberts Drive
  • - This road changes names to Chamblee Dunwoody Road. Continue a few miles
  • - We're a block past Mt Vernon Rd on the Left in the North Dekalb Cultural Center

Meet the Cast of Lend Me a Tenor

Karen Whitaker

Karen Whitaker is thrilled to be back at Stage Door with such a wonderful and hilarious cast and another Ken Ludwig farce (her last Ludwig stint was as Charlotte Hay in SDP's Moon Over Buffalo). Her last several roles include Grace in SDP's Bus Stop (for which she won a Woodie Award), Mrs. Darbus in High School Musical, Martha in Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?, Doris in Same Time, Next Year, and Mame in Mame. Karen was also honored to play the infamous actress, Tallulah Bankhead, at the Spoleto Festival in Charleston, SC.

Over her 26 years in Atlanta theatre, some of her other favorite credits include Five Women Wearing the Same Dress (Trisha), Parallel Lives - The Kathy and Mo Show (a two-woman show in which she played 17 different characters), Second Samuel (Omaha Nebraska Madison and Jimmy Deanne), Della's Diner - Blue Plate Special (Della), Best Little Whorehouse in Texas (Miss Mona), I Do! I Do! (Agnes), A Little Night Music (Desiree), Singin' in the Rain (Lina Lamont and, much later, Dora Bailey), and Mating Habits of the Urban Mammal (Martha). By day, you can find her leading process improvement at Georgia Transmission Corporation.
