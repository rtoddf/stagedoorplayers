Robert Egizio

Producing Artistic Director

Contact Robert

Originally from Philadelphia, and an alumni of the Temple University Theater school, Robert has called Atlanta home for almost 20 years. Prior to his arrival in Atlanta, he spent a number of years working and performing around the country before settling in South Florida where he was the Choreographer in Residence at Encore Alley in Vero Beach for four seasons.

As an actor, his credits include performances locally, nationally and on tour, with performances onstage in the Atlanta area with The Aurora Theater, Georgia Ensemble Theater, ART Station, Onstage Atlanta, Jewish Theater of the South and right here at Stage Door Players.

As a director and choreographer, Robert has been involved with numerous theater companies in the Atlanta area. He served as Choreographer in Residence for Actors Express under Chris Coleman, and has worked with ART Station, the Aurora Theatre, Gainesville Theater Alliance, Georgia Ensemble Theater, Georgia Perimeter College, The Lyric, and Onstage Atlanta.

About to embark on his 10th Anniversary Season, Robert's first involvement with Stage Door Players came in 2003, when he was hired to direct and choreograph Dames At Sea. He returned the following season to direct and choreograph Ain't Misbehavin' which would go on to win the theater's annual Woodie Award for Best Show of the Year. Due to the success of that show, Robert was brought on as the new Artistic Director, and in 2005 became the first full time Producing Artistic Director in the theater's history. Over the course of the next two seasons, Stage Door made the transition from a community theater group to a fully professional company, increasing the number of Season Ticket holders from 239 in 2004 to over 1000 last season.

As Stage Door Players begins its 40th Anniversary Season, Robert would like to take this opportunity to thank the, Board of Directors, Season Subscribers, community partners, volunteers, artists, audience members, and sponsors for allowing him to do what he loves so very much.

George Deavours

Wig Designer in Residence

Originally from Tennessee, George grew up around movies and theatres. His father was a manager for ABC Theaters, moving the family to several cities around the south before coming to Atlanta in 1970 as the city manager for the companies’ theatres, primarily the Fox.

George finished high school at Sequoyah HS in Doraville, and then attended Auburn University for two years before transferring to the University of TN, Knoxville, where he was a theatre major.  George had taught himself to cut and style hair at 15 and later became a licensed hairstylist. Wanting to move back toward entertainment, he acquired the position of Assistant Talent Relations Coordinator for the 1982 World’s Fair. In 1983, he was Message Center coordinator and a hairstylist for the Miss USA Pageant. Later that year, he moved back to Atlanta, and quickly established himself as a very busy stylist.

In 1989, at the urging of friends, George finally took the leap, and began performing on stage. After only one appearance on stage, he was cast as Harold Hill in The Music Man and has never looked back. He has performed in over 60 shows, and still loves the stage as much as he did on his first outing. Performances in Atlanta theatres include Atlanta Lyric Theatre, Onstage Atlanta, Theatre Gael, Neighborhood Playhouse, and of course, Stage Door Players. Some Stage Door performances include Dancing at Lughnasa, High Society, Bye Bye Birdie, and Clue: the Musical. George began work as a hairstylist/wigmaster/makeup artist for the stage in 1987. His work has been seen in over 170 shows in the southeast, CA, and NY, including Stage Door Players (beginning in 1993), Atlanta Lyric Theatre, Aurora Theatre, Actor’s Express, Georgia Ensemble Theatre, OnStage Atlanta, Legacy Theatre, Serenbe Playhouse, The Process Theatre, and the Springer Opera House.

Rial Ellsworth

Sound Designer in Residence

Whenever he is not on stage, Rial enjoys being part of the incredible production team here at Stage Door. Being a child of the sixties, he has always been fascinated with the effect music and sound have on human emotions. His background includes experience as a broadcaster, audio engineer and theatrical sound designer. His first job in radio came when he was just 14 years old. There he was introduced to the basics of “old school” analog sound manipulation:  reel-to-reel recorders, a razor blade and splicing tape. In later years he and his wife Kathy formed a sound company which provided live sound reinforcement for everything from orchestras to heavy metal bands in venues ranging from strip clubs to civic auditoriums. The technology has changed dramatically over the years, but the joy of seeing how sound touches an audience is still very much in his heart. He hopes you will enjoy listening to the show as much as you enjoy seeing it!

Jane Kroessig

Wardrobe Designer in Residence

Jane B. Kroessig began her love of costuming during Parents Weekend at Cedar Crest College in Allentown, PA. It was there that she designed her very first costume, an orange velour Snoopy costume she built for her best friend (yes, orange velour...because that was all that she could afford). The costume was a hit, and sparked an interest (and talent for accommodating any size production budget) that would eventually lead to a career. In 2004, the artistic director of OffShoot Productions met Jane, and took a chance on an unknown, but enthusiastic, seamstress to design and produce the costumes for Comedy of Errors. Thus, the journey began, and the chaos continues.

Jane is delighted to be returning to Stage Door after working as a freelance costume designer costuming nearly 100 shows for many Atlanta area theatres including The Essential Theatre, OnStage Atlanta, Serenbe Playhouse and The Legacy Theatre. She has been the recipient of two Metropolitan Atlanta Theatre Awards and has been nominated eight times. Some of her favorite costume productions include: Jane Eyre, The Games Afoot or Holmes for the Holidays, Side Show, and Young Frankenstein. She is originally from New Jersey, but has lived in Peachtree City, Georgia, for over 30 years with her husband, Tom. Jane holds a BFA and elementary education degree from Cedar Crest College, as well as the honor of having creating their only orange velour Snoopy.

Charles Welcome

Set Designer/Scenic Artist in Residence

Chuck began his illustrious career at Stage Door Players in January 2003, as the Set Designer for Agatha Christies, The Mousetrap, and has been here ever since. In addition the being the Scenic Designer in residence in September of 2004, he began working as the theaters Marketing Director, to aid and support the efforts of recently hired Artistic Director, Robert Egizio.

Chuck has worked in theaters all over the Atlanta Metro area and his works include the scenic design for the Southeast regional premiere of Ken Ludwig's new comedy Leading Ladies, the US premier of Londos D’Arrigo’s Spreading it Around as well as collaboration in conceiving an original holiday show Home for the Holidays, Design Assistant for My Fair Lady and Props Coordinator for the world professional premier of Disney's High School Musical, with Theater of the Stars.

Chuck has a background in art, interior design and marketing and by day he works as Regional Director for Upscale Showplaces Inc, staging, showcasing and managing vacant homes for sale in the Atlanta Metro. Stage Door Players has been his home away from home for 10 years now and counting and thrilled to be a part of SDP’s growth and continues to strive to create the best possible sets for our audiences and the talented people who come to play here!

Hampton Whatley

Production Manager

Contact Hampton

Hampton Whatley was born and raised in Atlanta (and no accent!). He has worked in the professional theatrical community since 1996. First working on stage with companies such as A.R.C. H. Productions, Dad’s Garage, Jewish Theatre of the South, Synchronicity Performance Group and Georgia Ensemble Theatre. He has also worked on camera for the Emmy winning Connecting with Kids network and various smaller commercial projects with WSB, Bellsouth, and Kroger. He moved from onstage to behind the scenes in 2005 for Jewish Theatre of the South and began Stage Managing for other professional theatres such as Center Theatre where he later became the Production Manager, Theatre in the Square, Pinch N’ Ouch and Aurora Theatre. He holds a strong background in Teen-Theatrical direction as the former Associate Artistic Director for A.R.C.H. Productions, directing Project Impact Theatre through Jewish Theatre of the South and also directing at Duluth High School.

He found his love for using tools at a young age and with the assistance of other great technical director talents, He was provided with the proper direction and the wonderful advice of a tetanus shot. He found a new home with Stage Door Players in 2010 stage managing for an a amazing set of designers. At that point I was hooked and Robert could not get rid of him if he tried. On as the Production Manager, he continuously provides support for the design and anything else needed to make Stage Door Players a theatrical center in Atlanta. Hampton holds a Bachelors in Business Administration, Masters in Project Management as well as a Masters in Public Administration. He currently is enrolled at Georgia Piedmont Technical College for a Diploma in Welding and Joining Technology. Ka-Kaw.

John David Williams

Lighting Designer in Residence

JD is a native Georgian and Dunwoody resident who joined the Stage Door Players for the 2008-2009 season. He got his start designing lights at South Georgia College using equipment that looked like it was installed when the school was chartered in 1906 and is happy to be working with much newer controls and fixtures here.

Prior to joining the, JD has served as technical director for Galaxy Music Theater and lighting director for Neighborhood Playhouse. JD has also designed shows for Onstage Atlanta and Macon Little Theatre. He has received numerous design awards around Atlanta since moving to the area in 1990. His first design for SDP was for Wrong Turn at Lungfish. Favorite shows here include Master Class, Pippin (co-design with Michael W. Magursky), Six Dance Lessons in Six Weeks and Visiting Mister Green. Other designs include Dream Girls and Smokey Joe's Cafe with Galaxy Music Theatre, Angels in America, K2 and Titanic (in concert) at Onstage Atlanta, Into the Woods and Singin’ in the Rain at Neighborhood Playhouse.

Also a percussionist, JD has played in the pit for several shows here at SDP. He has been seen and heard performing throughout the Southeastern US in major malls, parades and wedding receptions from Charleston, SC to Jackson, MS with The Seed & Feed Marching Abominable. He also works with direct marketing databases and is "an expert" in arcane USPS Postal Regulations.

Jacob York

Artistic Associate

A native of Benton, KY, Jacob moved to Atlanta, GA seven years ago to participate in the Shakespeare Tavern's Apprentice Program. Since then, he's been working as an actor, including roles such as Macbeth (The Atlanta Shakespeare Company), Algernon in The Importance of Being Earnest (Tennessee Repertory Theatre), Bennett Riggs in Angry Fags (7 Stages), Tubbs in Middle School: The Musical (Alliance Theatre), Tom in Fat Pig (Aurora Theatre), and Harry "Jazzbo" Heywood and Felix Geisel here at Stage Door Players (It's A Wonderful Life: A Live Radio Play and The Game's Afoot, respectively). In addition to acting, Jacob is a playwright whose first show, Homers, will be premiering at the Georgia Ensemble Theatre & Conservatory in early 2015.

Michael Magursky

Board President

Michael W. Magursky fell in love with the theatre while in high school in Jacksonville, FL. Though he enjoys being onstage, his real passion is with the technical aspects of theatre.

He has designed sets, video/multimedia, and both physical and pyrotechnic special effects, but, most often, you can find him behind a light board. Dr. Magursky has designed for a wide range of projects ranging from small church productions to large touring shows and from scholastic environments to professional theatre.

Some of his favorite lighting projects include: Ruthless! (Theatre Memphis), Tales of Lost Formicans (Generic Theatre, Norfolk, VA), Pageant (Galaxy Music Theatre), Little Shop of Horrors (Atlanta Lyric Theatre), Songs for A New World, Turn of the Screw (Onstage Atlanta), A Grand Night for Singing, and Pippin (Stage Door Players, Dunwoody, GA). He recently won a Woody Award for the lighting design of last season’s I Love You, You’re Perfect, Now Change.

Four years ago, Michael joined the board of directors of Stage Door Players and has headed up the Krewe of Thalia and Melpomene Mardi Gras fundraisers for the past two years.

When not pushing keys on a light board, hanging from a ladder or slinging Mardi Gras beads, Michael is a dentist with Peachtree Suwanee Dental Associates.
